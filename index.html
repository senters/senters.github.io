Abstract

   Senters is a user-level message-based protocol for narrative
   process scaffolding (NPS), a systems design pattern to facilitate
   improvised collaborative organization of augmented experiences.
   NPS involves the use of instruments, events, centers, and proxies.

   Users instrument their environments for effects and for
   information, in the familiar kappa event-driven architectural
   style.  Instruments are minimal utilities that track and emit
   events and in some cases change the state of the environment in so
   doing.  Instruments are controlled entirely by events, appending
   responded-to and emitted events onto immutable logs.

   Events can be direct, or they can be about other events, the latter
   of which is provided for with a simple gesture language.  Gestures
   signal the bearing of an event on one of the three predicaments of
   narrative process: engagement (engage or refrain), support (follow
   or voice), and realization (exit or continue).  When gesturing
   about the bearing of an event on process, the gesture event is
   committed to a log for that active process.

   The collected data of one's own and other people's processes forms
   the basis of centers, which are event-sourced claims of utility in
   situtations.  Centers are the means by which agents coordinate on
   process, or in other words scaffold shared end-directed activities.
   Any narrative process that one or more agents iterate on developing
   produces a center.

   Proxies are automatic generic artificial agents that users deploy
   to carry out any scaffolding they are confident in off-loading.
   Proxies attempt to optimize bringing relevant centers to the
   attention of their users in the appropriate circumstances,
   suggesting or carrying out supporing activities when a user is
   engaged in a particular narrative process, and calling attention to
   and discovering exit conditions to centers indicated as objectives.
